Roadside-stop `notes` must NOT be worded with respect to travel in one
particular direction — a route can be followed either way.

**Why:** the user may drive a corridor in either direction, so phrasing like
"the first stop after X", "before the climb to Y", "an easy first break east
of Z" is wrong half the time.

**How to apply:** describe the stop and its location/facilities neutrally —
e.g. "On US-6 in <town>" or "<town>'s city park, just off the IA-2 bypass" —
not "first stop heading east." Anchor to a town / highway / landmark, not a
direction of travel. (Pre-existing notes with directional wording, e.g. the
Crystal Spring I-70 stop, predate this and can be de-directionalized when next
touched.) Relates to [[feedback_attribute_note_edits]].
